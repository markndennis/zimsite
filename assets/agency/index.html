Tom

the first human to ever come to Hara

Born in 19th century London, the young genius Thomas Evening spent his early years on Earth as an outcast.  At the age of 20, one of Thomas’s failed experiments destroys a city block, resulting in his imprisonment as a “danger to society”; however, isolation in prison allows Thomas to focus on his work, tracing calculations in the dust across his cell. Recognizing his creative potential, The Kensei chose Thomas as a competitor in the 734th Conflux - making him the first human to ever come to Hara. Proving himself to be a Shui-wielding prodigy, Thomas finds success as an underdog in his first year -- earning himself a host of both friends and enemies.

Kaoru and Kuma

a hero not a victim

Kaoru hails from a war-torn and barbaric world that is governed by a ruthless matriarchy. All of the men in Kaoru’s tribe are hunters, each receiving their own bear companion after completing the trial of manhood. Kaoru, however, was born the scrawny runt of the litter, and suffered the loss of his left hand in a childhood accident. Handicapped and deemed weak, Kaoru was not even allowed to participate in the trial of manhood, resulting in constant bullying from his fellow tribesmen. Once pulled into Hara, Kaoru discovers he is able to use his affinity for Shui to construct his own bear companion, Kuma, and a powerful new hand made of stone. The young competitor seeks to win the Conflux to prove to himself that he is a hero - not a victim.

Sof

wild and rambunctious

Wild and rambunctious, Sof is chosen as a Champion for her sure-footedness and exuberance for adventure. Due to the violent nature in which Sof’s abilities manifest on Hara, her participation in the Conflux is met with opposition; frequently, several disgruntled Champions gang up to eliminate her in the early rounds -- although they never get away without a few bruises. During her time on Hara, Sof has brought countless complaints to the Kensei, pleading with them to bring Bamboo, her dog-dragon companion, to live on her new home. Continually refused, Sof hopes to win the Conflux in order to gain the power needed to be reunited with her best friend.  

Leaf

Leaf is a tree of few words

Unlike his fellow Champions, Leaf was not pulled into Hara from another world; rather, he was the product of a massive green rift, so large and violent that it split the once Great Forest into the separate islands Oakenmoor and The Baiyu. Because of the destructive nature of his birth, Leaf feels a heavy sense of guilt and responsibility, desperately trying to win the Conflux in order to gain the strength needed to fuse the two planes back together.  These days, Leaf is a tree of few words, preferring his beats over conversation. When he’s not listening to the natural rhythms of the forest, Leaf composes his own tunes in his bioluminescent beat-laboratory at the heart of the Baiyu.

Persephone

the being with the worst nightmares in the entire universe

When searching for new competitors, the Kensei do their best to avoid worlds filled with darkness and evil -- those types of environments typically don’t make for great Champions. Unfortunately, The Oneroi, a group of ninja-scientists dedicated to the study of nightmares, found their way into Hara on the merits of their extreme curiosity. Since their arrival, The Oneroi have managed to subtly tamper with the Kensei’s world-map, causing the Kensei to inadvertently search for the being with the worst nightmares in the entire universe -- Persephone. From the moment she was pulled into Hara, The Oneroi have tried to capture Persephone in hopes of studying her brain; however, while fleeing the organization’s shadowy agents, Persephone stumbles upon Thomas Evening’s newly constructed tower. After being offered protection, friendship, and food by the young scientist, Persephone decides to stay.

Orc

a talented painter and chef

King of the Painted City, Oppenheim, Orc is a man of high ideals, believing that the greatest forces in the universe emanate from the belly. A talented painter and chef, Orc insists that wielding Shui is an artist’s work, and that the only thing an artist requires is a full stomach. After witnessing Thomas’s unexpected victory, Orc takes a liking to the human and the two become fast friends and training partners. As king of Oppenheim, Orc feels responsible for using any energy he gains from competing in the Conflux to replenish the city’s arcane battery - the source of it’s inhabitants’ colorful magic.
